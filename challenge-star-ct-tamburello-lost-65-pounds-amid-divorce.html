'Challenge' star CT Tamburello lost 65 pounds amid divorce

“The Challenge” star Chris “CT” Tamburello revealed he shed a significant amount of weight during his messy divorce.

“I lost 65 f–king pounds,” the reality star said in a rambling, nearly 10-minute video posted to Instagram Friday.

Tamburello, 42, shared that he wants to look like Hollywood hunks George Clooney, Sean Connery and Michael Keaton with “salt-and-pepper hair” while being a “stay-at-home dad” to his 7-year-old son, Christopher Jr., whom he shares with his estranged wife, Lilianet “Lili” Solares.

“Now I’m gonna stay at home,” he emphasized. “That’s it.”

Even though the MTV personality was the one to file for divorce in November 2022, he claimed he was left wanting answers as to what went wrong in his four-year marriage.

“I have so many unresolved issues, so many unanswered questions, and if we’re gonna be better parents, I need some answers,” he said, speaking directly to Solares in the since-deleted video, which a fan reposted on Reddit.

Tamburello also accused his soon-to-be ex-wife of “living a secret life” with a “stripper from ‘Magic Mike.'”

“This guy right now probably thinks I’m the biggest piece of s–t,” he said of Solares’ alleged new man.

“Little does he know, my son, he’s over here every day telling me, ‘Dad, I wish I could live with you because it’s uncomfortable at Mom’s place,'” the “Real World: Paris” star claimed.

Tamburello concluded his rant by alleging that Solares does not let their son leave his bedroom anytime “Mom has a friend over.”

“Look, Lili, I don’t need you to tell me to be a better parent. I know I’m the best f–king parent, and my whole life revolves around [Christopher Jr.],” he said. “You got a lot of nerve trying to tell me to be better when you can’t even afford to put food on your table. What does that mean? Let’s be honest.”

Solares, who does not appear to have social media, has not addressed the allegations.

Since the couple separated, they have battled over custody, alimony and child support.

Us Weekly reported in March that Solares agreed to move out of her and Tamburello’s home and into a rental property, for which he contributed $7,2000. He was also ordered to pay her $761 a month.
