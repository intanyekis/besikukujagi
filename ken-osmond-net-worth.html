American actor Ken Osmond has an estimated net worth of $500 thousand. He is best recognized for his portrayal of role in the 1950s TV comedy Leave It to Beaver as Eddie Haskell. He also played the same role in the show's revival series titled The New Leave It to Beaver.

Kenneth Charles Osmond was born on June 7, 1943  in Glendale, California, USA. Since 1969, he has been married to Sandra Purdy  with whom he has two sons named Eric (b. 1971) and Christian (b. 1974).

Ken Osmond's acting career started when he was four. At age nine, he had his first speaking role in the film So Big, which topbilled Sterling Hayden and Jane Wyman. Subsequently, he appeared in films like Everything But the Truth and Good Morning Miss Dove. He also appeared as a guest-star on TV shows like The Loretta Young Show, Circus Boy, The Adventures of Ozzie and Harriet, Lassie, and Fury.  He also appeared on other TV series like  The Munsters and Petticoat Junction.

Ken Osmond was also featured in films like With Six You Get Eggroll and C'mon Let's Live a Little.
