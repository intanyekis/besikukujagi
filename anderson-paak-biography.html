Who is Anderson Paak?

Anderson Paak is an American musician and record producer. Paak is known as one-half of ‘NxWorries’, alongside record producer Knxwledge.

Additionally, he has released several solo albums over the years including ‘Venice’ and ‘Malibu.’

Anderson Paak: Age, Parents, Ethnicity

Anderson Paak was born Brandon Paak Anderson in Oxnard, California on February 8, 1986. As of 2021, he is 35 years old.

Anderson is of American nationality and mixed(African-American and Korean) ethnic background. His mother owned a business.

He had quite a rough childhood and he witnessed his estranged father attack his mother when he was just seven years old.

Anderson Paak mother remarried and they lived with his mother and stepfather. His father, a mother, as well as stepfather, was later arrested. He has sisters.

He likes dancing and drawing when he was young.

Later, when he was around 12 years old, he was intrigued by the world of music and wanted to play saxophones. His stepdad had a drum so he used to love playing it. From 12 to 28 age, he was a lead drummer at the church.

Paak began producing music from his bedroom as a teenager.

Career Journey of Anderson Paak

Anderson Paak was initially a drummer at his family’s church. Additionally, he also worked at a marijuana farm in Santa Barbara during the early part of his career.

He has also worked as an assistant, videographer, editor, writer, and producer for Shafiq Husayn.

Eventually, he released his debut album titled ‘Venice’ on October 28, 2014. With ‘NxWorries’, Anderson has released two albums namely, ‘Yes Lawd!’ and ‘Yes Lawd! Remixes.’

Anderson Paak’s studio albums are ‘O.B.E. Vol. 1’, ‘Lovejoy’, ‘Venice’, ‘Malibu’, and ‘Oxnard.’

Furthermore, he has also collaborated with other popular bands and artists like Mac Miller, Rapsody, Westside Gunn, Chance the Rapper, Keith Ape, Macklemore, Ryan Lewis, Eminem, Candice Pillay, and Dr. Dre.

He announced a world tour in support of Oxnard on December 3, 2018.

Awards

Anderson Paak garnered the BET Hip Hop Award nomination in 2016 in the category of Best New Hip Hop Artist. Additionally, he has also received three Grammy Award nominations.

In 2016, Anderson won the Centric Certified Award in Soul Train Music Award.
