Bryce Dallas Early Life

Cheryl Howard, a writer, and Ron Howard, an actor and director, welcomed Bryce Dallas Howard into the world on March 2, 1981 in Los Angeles, California. She has a younger brother named Reed as well as twin sisters Jocelyn and Paige. Bryce is the niece of actor Clint Howard and the granddaughter of actors Rance Howard and Jean Speegle Howard through her father. Actor Henry Winkler, who starred alongside her father in the American comedy television series Happy Days from the 1970s to the 1980s, is her godfather.

Howard grew up on a farm in Greenwich, Connecticut and in Westchester County, New York.
Howard and her siblings were not exposed to the entertainment industry while growing up because their parents forbade them from watching television and instead encouraged them to engage in outdoor activities and hobbies.

She was allowed to appear in her father’s movies as an extra when she was seven years old. She claimed that family friend Tom Cruise frequently babysat her and her siblings during a 2017 appearance on Watch What Happens Live with Andy Cohen.

Alongside Natalie Portman, Howard received his acting instruction at Stagedoor Manor, a performing arts camp in upstate New York.

After graduating from Byram Hills High School in 1999 and attending Greenwich Country Day School until 1996, Howard studied for three years at the Tisch School of the Arts at New York University (NYU), taking courses at the Stella Adler Studio of Acting, the Experimental Theatre Wing, and the International Theatre Workshop in Amsterdam.

Without finishing her degree, she took a leave of absence from NYU to pursue roles.

Bryce Dallas Career

Howard made several theatrical appearances in New York early in her acting career, including “House & Garden,” “Tartuffe,” and Shakespeare’s “As You Like It.” M. Night Shyamalan was impressed by her performance in the latter and decided to cast Howard in his fantasy thriller “The Village” in 2004. She portrays Ivy, the movie’s main female character who tries to venture into the forbidden outside world and is the blind daughter of the village chief.

Then, Howard played Grace Margaret Mulligan in Lars von Trier’s drama “Manderlay,” the follow-up to the director’s “Dogville,” taking Nicole Kidman’s place. In addition to resuming her stage role as Rosalind in Kenneth Branagh’s film adaptation of “As You Like It” in 2006, Howard collaborated once more with Shyamalan on the paranormal thriller “Lady in the Water.”

After that, Howard became a household name when she was cast as Gwen Stacy in the superhero epic “Spider-Man 3” in 2007. She played the lead in the independent film “The Loss of a Teardrop Diamond” the following year. The 2009 science-fiction action movie “Terminator Salvation,” in which Howard starred as Katherine Brewster Connor opposite Christian Bale’s John Connor, marked Howard’s return to blockbuster Hollywood filmmaking. Howard played additional significant roles in “The Twilight Saga: Eclipse” and Clint Eastwood’s fantasy disaster film “Hereafter” in 2010.

2011 saw Howard play important supporting roles in both the dramedy “50/50” and the Kathryn Stockett novel adaptation “The Help.” She portrays Hilly Holbrook in the former, a racist socialite who ran the Junior League chapter in Mississippi in 1963.

The movie was a huge box office and critical hit, and Howard won the Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture for his work in the ensemble cast. After that, the actress landed one of her biggest roles to date in the blockbuster sequel “Jurassic World,” playing the operations manager of the film’s titular dinosaur theme park opposite Chris Pratt.

In the sequels “Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom” and “Jurassic World: Dominion,” Howard played the same character again. She also starred in the crime drama “Gold,” the 2016 remake of “Pete’s Dragon,” and the musical biopic “Rocketman,” in which she played Elton John’s mother.

On television, Howard directed a segment of the 2013 Lifetime anthology series “Call Me Crazy: A Five Film.” A few years later, in the “Nosedive” episode of the science-fiction anthology series “Black Mirror,” she gave one of her most acclaimed performances, portraying a woman in the near future who is driven insane by a system that assigns social ratings to each person. For her performance, Howard received a Screen Actors Guild Award nomination.

Later, in 2019 and 2020, Howard took over the director’s chair for a number of “The Mandalorian” episodes on Disney+.

On “The Book of Boba Fett” on Disney+, she directed once more.

About Her Person Life

She first met actor Seth Gabel at New York University, where Howard was a student. Before getting married in June 2006, the couple dated for five years. She became pregnant with their first child just a week after their wedding, and she gave birth to a son named Theodore. The couple welcomed a daughter named Beatrice in 2012. 2019 saw Howard and her family relocate from Santa Monica, California to an eco-friendly home in upstate New York that had once been William Hurt’s home.
